GPI Proteome Scan — I. scapularis
===================================

The UniProt keyword search (KW-0336, 'GPI-anchored membrane protein')
identified 13 experimentally annotated GPI-anchored
proteins in I. scapularis (taxon 6945). A computational heuristic scan of
the reviewed proteome using the C-terminal hydrophobic signal motif (Eisenhaber
et al. 1998) predicted an additional 3 candidates.
Based on the conserved ~1–3% prevalence of GPI-anchored proteins in arthropod
proteomes, an estimated 200–600 I. scapularis proteins
are predicted to carry GPI anchors and would be affected by PGAP5/Cdc1 inhibition.

Biological significance: PGAP5/Cdc1 performs the final remodeling step of all
GPI anchors before surface display. Inhibition would broadly disrupt surface
protein expression, including adhesion proteins, immune evasion factors, and
complement regulators critical for successful tick feeding. The Bm86 antigen —
the antigen in the only commercially licensed tick vaccine — is GPI-anchored,
demonstrating that GPI-anchored surface proteins are validated immunological
and pharmacological targets in tick biology.

Paper-ready sentence:
"Inhibition of PGAP5/Cdc1 (B7P5E9) would potentially disrupt GPI-anchor
remodeling for an estimated 200–600 surface proteins
in I. scapularis, including adhesion, immune evasion, and complement-resistance
factors critical for tick feeding competence."
